# Phase 2: Discover

Research the competitors and identify the comparison dimensions. This is
the evidence-gathering phase; the draft comes in Phase 3.

## 2.1 Identify Competitors

From the scoped slice (Phase 1), enumerate the competitors that belong in
the survey. Include the ones that genuinely contend on the competitive
question; exclude the ones the scope boundary pushes out, and note why a
notable name was excluded if a reader might expect it.

## 2.2 Identify Dimensions

Determine the dimensions along which competitors are compared — the axes
that actually matter to the competitive question (for example: speed,
ergonomics, price, lock-in, extensibility). The dimensions are the
backbone of the analysis: the Market Overview names them, the
Comparative Matrix columns are them, and per-competitor findings are
stated against them. Pick dimensions that discriminate; an axis on which
every competitor scores the same teaches nothing.

## 2.3 Research Each Competitor

For each competitor, gather concrete, sourced observations against each
dimension. Capture at least one real strength and one real weakness per
competitor — a one-sided picture is a pitch, not a survey. Record the
source for every non-obvious claim, with the date it was checked, since
competitive facts go stale.

Use web research and any available evidence. Keep observations factual
and dimension-anchored; defer judgment to the Implications section in
Phase 3.

## 2.4 Load the Format Reference

Read `references/comp-format.md` before drafting so the research maps
cleanly onto the required sections and the quality guidance.

## Output

Per-competitor research notes (strengths, weaknesses, sourced
observations against each dimension) and the finalized dimension list,
captured in your working notes for Phase 3 to draft from.
